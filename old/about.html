About Me

       My name is David O'Regan, and I am currently a senior in high school. I was first introduced to programming during the summer before freshman year, at an iD Tech + VA Tech Java camp. During that camp, I discovered a new passion for game and software development. My growing passion for programming led me to take AP & IB Computer Science through high school, as well as extra courses with MIT and iD Tech. On 2 June 2022, I earned the NOCTI Knowledge-Based Workforce Competency Credential for passing the NOCTI Computer Programming industry-based certification. The projects that I have created can be found in the Portfolio tab.

       In 2019 and 2020, I was a member of the George Mason Game Programming Club, and it was there that I learned about Unity and the work that goes into the process of game development. In 2021, I joined FRC Team 1418 as a lead programmer. During the recent season (2022) of the FIRST Robotics Competition, I was in charge of creating the Driver Dashboard (software that makes driving the robot easier) and helped with the autonomous robot code.
